My name is Simon and I make websites. Kinda. It's a bit more complicated than that – the things I make tend to be a fair bit more involved than just words on a screen – but that's what I tell people when I don't want to bore them too much.

Professionally I tend to work on data-heavy frontends, often producing large scale visualisations or professional quality CRUD interfaces. That stuff tends to be both copyright and slightly dull though so on this site I'm putting the fun bits I work on in my free time.

Welcome!

Recently I've been working on getting good with ThreeJS. I shamelessly dissected the "flocking" example1 until I understood it well enough to build a box full of butterflies2. I've then taken that learning and applied it to the page you're on now, streamlining the 3D "physics" to a much less complicated 2D vesion that still gives me all the control I need (for now). It's very much a work-in-progress, eventually I'll have all kinds of stuff flying around!

I'm also working on-and-off on a cave-based game2 to really push my abilities. Frankly it's still in the early design stages, while I have a rough idea what I want to build I need to put thought into things like objectives and purpose within the game. There's also tons of modelling, animation, texturing, coding, etc. etc. to do too, so I'm sure to have plenty to keep me busy. Heck at some point I need to think about audio! I'm open to any and all feedback and suggestions so have a go on the demo and let me know what you think.

I've dabbled with 3D rendering before getting into ThreeJS. Here's the platonic solids rotating in 3-space, rendered using SVG. I used the same renderer to make this interpretation of the concept (give it a click!) It lacks some of the finesse of WebGL but it really helped me to understand a lot of important concepts.

Probably the best thing I made through this technique3 was a "play" button. It's fun to click and actually almost makes sense to use over a more "full fat" solution like webgl. It would also be relatively easy to extend to different icons etc., maybe one day I'll use it in production (there's one bottom right that's a strong candidate).
